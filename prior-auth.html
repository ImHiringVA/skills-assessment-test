Section A: Multiple Choice (1 point each)

Select the BEST answer. Some questions have intentionally tricky options.

1A patient has a Blue Cross PPO plan. You call to verify benefits for an MRI of the cervical spine. The rep says PA is required. What is your IMMEDIATE next step?
2A PA is approved for CPT 72148 (MRI lumbar without contrast). The radiologist also performs CPT 72149 (with contrast). What is the risk?
3You verify eligibility and the portal shows: Status: Active | Effective: 01/01/2024 | Term: 12/31/2024 | EPO. Appointment is 03/15/2025. What do you do?
4What is the difference between a referral and a prior authorization?
5Patient has Medicare (primary) + Medigap Plan F (secondary). For a specialist visit, do you need PA from the Medigap plan?
6What does “retroactive authorization” mean?

8TRICK QUESTION: A PA approval guarantees the claim will be paid. True or false?

Section B: Short Answer (4 points each)

Be specific and detailed. One-sentence answers get zero.

9Walk through your COMPLETE process for verifying a patient’s insurance eligibility for a specialist visit. Include: info needed before calling, what you ask the payer, what you document, and what you do if there’s a problem.
10Explain the difference between these plan types and how each affects PA: HMO, PPO, EPO, POS, Medicare Advantage (HMO), and Original Medicare with Medigap.
11Submitting PA for a lumbar epidural steroid injection (CPT 62323). List EVERY piece of information needed. Be exhaustive.
12PA denied. You have 3 options: reconsideration, formal appeal, peer-to-peer review. Explain each, when to use it, and the process.
